Aluminum Alloy Specifications

Each aluminum alloy offers unique properties optimized for specific applications and performance requirements

6061-T6 Aluminum

Aerospace Grade

The most versatile and widely used heat-treatable aluminum alloy. Known for its excellent machinability, weldability, and corrosion resistance. Ideal for a wide range of applications from aerospace to consumer electronics.

Tensile Strength 45 ksi
Yield Strength 40 ksi
Density 0.0975 lb/in³
Machinability Excellent

Key Applications

  • Aircraft structural components
  • Automotive parts
  • Medical devices
  • Consumer electronics

7075-T6 Aluminum

High Strength

One of the highest-strength aluminum alloys available. Its strength-to-weight ratio is excellent, making it ideal for high-stress aerospace and defense applications.

Tensile Strength 83 ksi
Yield Strength 73 ksi
Density 0.102 lb/in³
Machinability Fair

Key Applications

  • High-stress aircraft components
  • Defense applications
  • High-performance molds
  • Sporting goods

2024-T3 Aluminum

Fatigue Resistant

High-strength alloy with excellent fatigue resistance. Widely used in aerospace structures, especially for tension-loaded components.

Tensile Strength 68 ksi
Yield Strength 47 ksi
Density 0.100 lb/in³
Machinability Good

Key Applications

  • Aircraft fuselage and wings
  • High-performance engine parts
  • Structural components
  • Automotive wheels

5083 Aluminum

Marine Grade

Exceptional performance in extreme environments, particularly seawater. Retains excellent strength after welding, making it ideal for marine and cryogenic applications.

Tensile Strength 47 ksi
Yield Strength 35 ksi
Density 0.096 lb/in³
Machinability Good

Key Applications

  • Marine and naval vessels
  • Cryogenic tanks
  • Pressure vessels
  • Chemical equipment
